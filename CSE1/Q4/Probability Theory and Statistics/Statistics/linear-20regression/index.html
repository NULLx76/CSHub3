Linear regression model

Let ﻿(x1,y1),…,(xn,yn)\left(x_1,y_1\right),\dots,\left(x_n,y_n\right)(x1​,y1​),…,(xn​,yn​)﻿ be a bivariate dataset.


A simple linear regression model: assume that ﻿x1,…,xnx_1,\dots,x_nx1​,…,xn​﻿ are non-random and ﻿y1,…,yny_1,\dots,y_ny1​,…,yn​﻿ are realization from random variables ﻿Yi=α+βxi+UiY_i=\alpha+\beta x_i+U_iYi​=α+βxi​+Ui​﻿ where ﻿UiU_iUi​﻿ (errors) are independent RVs with ﻿E[Ui]=0E\left[U_i\right]=0E[Ui​]=0﻿ and ﻿Var(Ui)=σ2Var\left(U_i\right)=\sigma^2Var(Ui​)=σ2﻿.


Regression line

The regression line tries to minimize the distance between the observed points and the line drawn. See this post for more on the least squares method, which we will use to minimize the following sum:

S(α,β)=∑i=1n(yi−α−βxi)2S(\alpha, \beta)=\sum_{i=1}^n(y_i-\alpha-\beta x_i)^2S(α,β)=∑i=1n​(yi​−α−βxi​)2

Here ﻿(xi,yi)\left(x_i,y_i\right)(xi​,yi​)﻿ is an observed value


Remarks:

  • Then, the line ﻿y=α+βxy=\alpha+\beta xy=α+βx﻿ is the regression line
  • Parameters ﻿α\alphaα﻿ and ﻿β\betaβ﻿ are the intercept and slope
  • ﻿yyy﻿ is called the response variable and ﻿xxx﻿ is called the explanatory variable. You can view this as ﻿xxx﻿ being a tall parent (explanatory), and thus ﻿yyy﻿ is s tall child (response)


Suppose the ﻿UiU_iUi​﻿ have a ﻿N(0,σ2)N\left(0,\sigma^2\right)N(0,σ2)﻿ distribution. Then the ﻿YiY_iYi​﻿ have a ﻿N(α+βxi,σ2)N\left(\alpha+\beta x_i,\sigma^2\right)N(α+βxi​,σ2)﻿ distribution.


Using the maximum likelihood procedure we can show that the likelihood L(α,β,σ)L(\alpha,\beta,\sigma)L(α,β,σ) attains its maximum when σ^2=1n−2∑i=1n(Yi−α^−β^xi)2\hat{\sigma}^2=\frac{1}{n-2}\sum^n_{i=1}(Y_i-\hat{\alpha}-\hat{\beta}x_i)^2σ^2=n−21​∑i=1n​(Yi​−α^−β^​xi​)2 is minimal. This σ^2\hat{\sigma}^2σ^2 is an unbiased estimator for σ2\sigma^2σ2

This yields the following MLE (maximum likelikhood estimate):

  • α^=yˉn−B^xˉn\hat{\alpha}=\bar{y}_n-\hat{\Beta}\bar{x}_nα^=yˉ​n​−B^xˉn​
  • β^=n∑ixiyi−(∑ixi)(∑iyi)n∑ixi2−(∑ixi)2\hat{\beta}=\frac{n\sum_ix_iy_i-(\sum_ix_i)(\sum_iy_i)}{n\sum_ix_i^2-(\sum_ix_i)^2}β^​=n∑i​xi2​−(∑i​xi​)2n∑i​xi​yi​−(∑i​xi​)(∑i​yi​)​


Matrices

Mapping this to a matrix gives the equation discussed in linear algebra:

  • ﻿(x1,y1)⇒y1=α+βxi+U1\left(x_1,y_1\right)\Rightarrow y_1=\alpha+\beta x_i+U_1(x1​,y1​)⇒y1​=α+βxi​+U1​﻿
  • ﻿(x2,y2)⇒y2=α+βx2+U2\left(x_2,y_2\right)\Rightarrow y_2=\alpha+\beta x_2+U_2(x2​,y2​)⇒y2​=α+βx2​+U2​﻿
  • etc.

This gives

[y1y2⋮yn]=[1x11x2⋮⋮1xn][αβ]+[U1U2⋮Un] \begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\ y_2 \\ \vdots \\ y_n \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & x_1 \\ 1 & x_2 \\ \vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & x_n \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \alpha \\ \beta \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} U_1 \\ U_2 \\ \vdots \\ U_n \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎢⎢⎢⎡​y1​y2​⋮yn​​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎤​=⎣⎢⎢⎢⎡​11⋮1​x1​x2​⋮xn​​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎤​[αβ​]+⎣⎢⎢⎢⎡​U1​U2​⋮Un​​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎤​

This can also be written as ﻿y=Xθ+Uy=X\theta+Uy=Xθ+U﻿, where:

  • ﻿yyy﻿ is the observation vector
  • ﻿XXX﻿ is the design matrix
  • ﻿θ\thetaθ﻿ is the parameter vector
  • ﻿UUU﻿ is the error vector


Parameter estimation

In this case, for the system y=Xθy=X\thetay=Xθ this yields the solution θ^=(XTX)−1XTy\hat{\theta}=(X^TX)^{-1}X^Tyθ^=(XTX)−1XTy.


Some remarks:

  • There is no assumption on the distribution of the errors, except the previously mentioned ﻿E[Ui]=0E\left[U_i\right]=0E[Ui​]=0﻿ and ﻿Var(Ui)=σ2Var\left(U_i\right)=\sigma^2Var(Ui​)=σ2﻿
  • This setting can easily be extended to the multivariate case. For instance with two explanatory variables ﻿X1X_1X1​﻿ and ﻿X2X_2X2​﻿ which would give the data ﻿(y1,x11,x21),…,(yn,x1n,x2n)\left(y_1,x_{11},x_{21}\right),\dots,\left(y_n,x_{1n},x_{2n}\right)(y1​,x11​,x21​),…,(yn​,x1n​,x2n​)﻿.


Residuals

The ﻿iii﻿th residual ﻿rir_iri​﻿ is defined as the vertical distance between the ﻿iii﻿th point and the estimated regression line.

Then we get ri=yi−α^−β^xir_i=y_i-\hat{\alpha}-\hat{\beta}x_iri​=yi​−α^−β^​xi​.

When a linear model is appropriate, a scatterplot of the ﻿rir_iri​﻿ versus the ﻿xix_ixi​﻿ should not show any trend or pattern.


How can we check that the ﻿UiU_iUi​﻿ have a ﻿N(0,σ2)N\left(0,\sigma^2\right)N(0,σ2)﻿ distribution? Then ﻿rir_iri​﻿ should look like a sample from a normal distribution.
