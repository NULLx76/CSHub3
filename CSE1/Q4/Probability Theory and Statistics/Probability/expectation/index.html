Expectation

The expectation of a random variable is the average of all the outcomes of doing an experiment a large amount of times. The expectation can be thought of as the center of mass.


The expectation ﻿E[X]E\left[X\right]E[X]﻿ of a discrete random variable ﻿XXX﻿ taking the values ﻿a1,a2,…a_1,a_2,\dotsa1​,a2​,…﻿ and with a probability mass function ﻿ppp﻿ is defined as the number:

﻿E[X]=∑iaiP(X=ai)=∑iaip(ai)E\left[X\right]=\sum_i^{ }a_iP\left(X=a_i\right)=\sum_i^{ }a_ip\left(a_i\right)E[X]=∑i​ai​P(X=ai​)=∑i​ai​p(ai​)﻿

This formula is basically taking the probabilities of the individual events and summing them.


The expectation ﻿E[X]E\left[X\right]E[X]﻿ of a continuous random variable ﻿XXX﻿ with probability density function ﻿fff﻿ is the number:

﻿E[X]=∫−∞∞xf(x)dxE\left[X\right]=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}xf\left(x\right)dxE[X]=∫−∞∞​xf(x)dx﻿


As the probabilities of individual values are all ﻿000﻿, we can't take the sum. Thus what we do is we take the sum of ﻿aia_iai​﻿ with a very small interval: ﻿E[X]=∑iaiP(ai≤X≤ai+Δa)E\left[X\right]=\sum_i^{ }a_iP\left(a_i\le X\le a_i+\Delta a\right)E[X]=∑i​ai​P(ai​≤X≤ai​+Δa)﻿ with ﻿Δa\Delta aΔa﻿ arbitrary low (thus using an integral)

Correlation vs covariance

Covariance indicates the direction of the linear relationship between variables. Correlation on the other hand measures both the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables. Correlation is a function of the covariance. What sets them apart is the fact that correlation values are standardized whereas, covariance values are not. This means that change-of-variable (taking for example ﻿rX+srX+srX+s﻿) has influence on the covariance, but not on the correlation. Covariance can take on values between ﻿−∞-\infty−∞﻿ and ﻿∞\infty∞﻿, whereas the correlation only takes values between ﻿−1-1−1﻿ and ﻿111﻿. You get ﻿±1\pm1±1﻿ when both random variables are linear functions.
