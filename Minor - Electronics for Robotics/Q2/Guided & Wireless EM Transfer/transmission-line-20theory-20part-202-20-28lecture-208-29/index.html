Generic complex load impedance

If we try to obtain a ﻿Z02Z_{02}Z02​﻿ for a complex load impedance, it will be complex. The ﻿Z02Z_{02}Z02​﻿ being complex means the line is not lossless, so this is not possible.


We can fix this by adding a:

  • Reactance equal and opposite to the load in series with the load
  • Susceptance equal and opposite to the load in parallel with the load


You can for example take a capacitor or inductor, depending on the sign of the impedance.

Note this will not completely cancel all impedance, only the complex part (but it may affect it). After the cancellation, we still use the previously described technique to match the impedance.


Depending on the application, the series example may more suitable, as it'll work on a wider bandwidth.


Short-circuited stub

A short-circuited stub effectively acts as a reactive impedance.

As you can see, the input impedance depends on the length of the wire, it is periodic with ﻿λ/4\lambda/4λ/4﻿.


Therefore there are always certain lengths that provide you with the correct input impedance.


Open-circuited stub

An open-circuited stub effectively acts as a reactive impedance, but opposite of the short-circuited stub.
