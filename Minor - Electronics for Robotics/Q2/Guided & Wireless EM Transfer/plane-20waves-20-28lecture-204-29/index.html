Plane waves

Electromagnetic waves

A time-varying electric field ﻿E(t)E\left(t\right)E(t)﻿ produces a time-varying magnetic field ﻿H(t)H\left(t\right)H(t)﻿, and vice-versa. This cyclic pattern generates electromagnetic waves:


Propagating is guided in a bounded medium (e.g. waveguide) or not guided in an unbounded medium (e.g. antenna transmission).


Energy is emitted by a source in the form of spherical waves, but if you (observer) are very far away the way looks like a uniform plane wave (because the circle is very large):

Real plane waves cannot exist because they would contain infinite energy. They are also called Transverse Electromagnetic waves (TEM).

This means, that E~\tilde \bm EE~, H~\tilde \bm HH~, and k^\hat \bm kk^ are always orthogonal to each other (they form an orthogonal triad):

They are plane waves:

  • Because they have an equi-phase surface they propagate in a certain direction, with a constant phase and amplitude.
  • Any generic field can be expressed as a sum of plane waves
  • Plane waves are TEM (Transverse ElectroMagnetic) waves - both ﻿EEE﻿ and ﻿HHH﻿ are orthogonal to the direction of propagation
