Circuit theory

For information about circuit theory, see the circuit analysis course. I will only write new things here:

  • If the wavelength is much larger than circuit size, the phase is approximately the same everywhere (quasi-static). This is when we can use linear circuits.
  • Impedance is a complex number: ﻿Z=R+jXZ=R+jXZ=R+jX﻿, with
  • ﻿R=ℜ{Z}R=\Re\left\{Z\right\}R=ℜ{Z}﻿, resistance
  • ﻿X=ℑ{Z}X=\Im\left\{Z\right\}X=ℑ{Z}﻿, reactance
  • Admittance is defined as the reciprocal of the impedance, ﻿Y=1Z=G+jSY=\frac{1}{Z}=G+jSY=Z1​=G+jS﻿, with:
  • ﻿G=ℜ{Y}G=\Re\left\{Y\right\}G=ℜ{Y}﻿, conductance
  • ﻿S=ℑ{Y}S=\Im\left\{Y\right\}S=ℑ{Y}﻿, susceptance
