L4.1: Which of the following statements is true?

  1. A plane wave has no electric or magnetic field component parallel to its direction of propagation.
  2. A plane wave has both the electric and magnetic field components parallel to its direction of propagation.
  3. A plane wave has always at least one field component (electric or magnetic) parallel to its direction of propagation.


L4.2: The plane waves are called in this way because

  1. The E\bm EE- and H\bm HH-fields and the direction of propagation k\bm kk are always parallel to the same planar surface.
  2. The E\bm EE- and H\bm HH-fields are constant on any arbitrary planar surface C. The E\bm EE- and H\bm HH-fields have constant phase over a planar surface.


L4.3: Which of the following statements is true?

  1. Plane waves are not a solution of the Maxwell equations, but they are useful to determine the real solution.
  2. Plane waves are solutions of the Maxwell equations.
  3. Plane waves are a solution of the Maxwell equations only in electro- and magneto-statics.


L4.4: Which of the following statements is true?

  1. Any generic field can be expressed as a sum of plane waves.
  2. Near to a source, the field behaves always as a plane wave.
  3. Near to a source, the field behaves always as the sum of two plane waves.

  1. Electric and magnetic fields have uniform properties on the direction of propagation.
