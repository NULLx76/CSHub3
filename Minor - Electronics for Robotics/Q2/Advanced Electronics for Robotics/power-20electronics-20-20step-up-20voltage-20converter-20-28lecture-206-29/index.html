The main assumption is that ﻿CCC﻿ is big enough that ﻿VoV_oVo​﻿ can be considered constant. If we keep increasing the resistance, then since the ﻿VoV_oVo​﻿ must stay constant, the current will keep decreasing. At some point, the current will hit 0 at the peak of the cycle, but not stay on 0. This is boundary conduction mode.

Bidirectional converter (Half-bridge converter)

There is the Buck converter (top, drops voltage) and the boost converter (bottom, increases voltage).

The only difference is that the diode/switch are flipped. We can combine them like this, to get a bidirectional power converter.


It is very useful in battery applications, you want to charge the battery using a Buck converter and then boost the voltage coming out of the battery:

Note that the voltage is always higher on the left side, it’s just that the power can now flow both ways.


If switch ﻿bbb﻿ is always open, it’s a Buck converter.

If switch ﻿uuu﻿ is always open, it’s a boost converter.
