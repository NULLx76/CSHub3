2.2 Sine waves

Some characteristics of sine waves:

  • The period of a sine wave is the wavelength and is the time between two similar points (i.e. max values)
  • Frequency ﻿fff﻿ is the inverse of period in Hz
  • The maximum amplitude ﻿AAA﻿ is also called the peek value (﻿VpV_pVp​﻿) in electronics.

  2. Peak factor = Peak value / RMS value

2.4 Measuring voltages and currents

Measuring

To measure the voltage between two points in a circuit, connect a voltmeter over a part of the circuit. The volt meter’s resistance should be as high as possible.


To measure the current flowing through a conductor or a component, we connect an ammeter in series with the element. The ammeter’s resistance should be as high as possible.


Calibration

Analog meters for sine waves are often calibrated to read the RMS of a sine wave. Since the form factor for sine waves is ﻿1.111.111.11﻿, readings are multiplied by ﻿1.111.111.11﻿ so that you can directly read the RMS value directly.


The reason for this is that you often want to use this RMS value because it also works for alternating waves.


This means that for other waves, such as square waves, you'd have to adjust your reading to read the proper voltage. Since the form factor of a square wave is ﻿111﻿ and the meter is calibrated for a form factor of ﻿1.111.111.11﻿, a ﻿10V10V10V﻿ wave would be read as ﻿101.11=9V\frac{10}{1.11}=9V1.1110​=9V﻿.
