EM waves

We use energy in Electro Magnetic waves to transport information without cables. This wireless communication is unguided transmission. The signals can be received at many locations.


Frequency bandwidth in the radio spectrum is a scarce resource and therefore expensive: it has to be used as efficiently as possible.


EM-waves propagate through the atmosphere with the speed of light (﻿c≈3⋅108m/sc\approx3\cdot10^8m/sc≈3⋅108m/s﻿).

Antennas

The antenna is a transducer which can go 2 ways (in and out). A few properties:

  • An electrical signal is converted into an electromagnetic wave and vice versa
  • It is the interface between a guided medium (usually a cable) and the free space

Cables

The signal can only be received at a location connected to the cable carrying the signal: cable communication is only applicable if the receiver location is known. The capacity of a cable depends on the type of cable and its length.

Noise

There are different sources of noise (fundamental physical phenomena limiting the performance of telecommunication systems):

  • Thermal resistor noise: due to the random thermal movement of electrons a noise voltage is generated.
  • Active components: shot noise, etc.
  • Signals received by antenna: atmosphere, man-made, interference


Without noise an infinitely small signal power would be sufficient for reliable communication: after "infinite" amplification reliable detection would be possible
