<p>A Turing Machines is a new model of computation which extends upon FSM's and CFL's. Turing recognizable languages are enough to perform all required operations for a computer. A TM will initially be at the leftmost cell, and can move left or right and read and write the current symbol.</p><p><br></p><p>We can describe TMs in a few ways:</p><ul><li><strong>Formal description: </strong>all details in terms of <span class="ql-formula" data-value="\delta">﻿<span contenteditable="false"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.69444em;
