<h1 id="sequential-systems">Sequential systems</h1><p>Sequential systems not only depend on the “current” input to determine the output, but also on the previous inputs.</p><p><br></p><p>We may even need all inputs since the start, but that may be impractical. Hence we use <strong>states </strong>to reflect a system's input history. The number of states is usually limited and thus its memory is less than what would be required had we saved all inputs.</p><p><br></p><p><strong>Synchronous systems: </strong>inputs and outputs are considered at discrete time instants that are defined by pulses of a clock.
