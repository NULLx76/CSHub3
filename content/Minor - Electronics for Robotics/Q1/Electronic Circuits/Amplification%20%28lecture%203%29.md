<h1 id="3.-amplifier">3. Amplifier</h1><p>An <strong>amplifier</strong> is any device that changes (usually increases) the amplitude of a signal. There is more power available at the output than taken from the input.</p><p><br></p><p>This is done by connecting a power source to an additional port.</p><p><img src="https://i.imgur.com/XLSOQuS.png" width="544"></p><p>The symbol of an amplifier is:</p><p><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Amplifier_symbol.svg/1200px-Amplifier_symbol.svg.png" width="159" alt="File:Amplifier symbol.svg - Wikimedia Commons"></p><h2 id="3.2-simple-amplifier-using-a-control-device">3.2 Simple amplifier using a control device</h2><p>A simple amplifier is a circuit using a <strong>control device</strong>, which has an input that gets changed by the control device. For example, a variable voltage divider.</p><p><br></p><p>We have a lamp on one side, and an LDR (resistor that decreases resistance based on the light strength). If the signal is strong, the LDR will have a lower resistance, but since the power supply has more power than the signal, we will amplify the signal power!</p><p><br></p><p><img src="https://i.imgur.com/FG5zJYh.png" width="439"></p><p><br></p><h2 id="3.3-solenoid-control-using-a-control-device">3.3 Solenoid control using a control device</h2><p>In a solenoid device we convert energy into an electric field, which then holds a weight.</p><p><br></p><p>There is a different hold current and lift current, we need a certain amount of current to get started, and after that we only need a fraction of it for the weight to remain lifted.</p><p><img src="https://i.imgur.com/k8TDz3G.png" width="316"></p><p>There is a flyback diode to protect the circuit from the inductor. The Arduino can control the transistor. It can turn the transistor on for a certain percentage of time (called a duty cycle) to control the power used.</p><p><br></p><p>Note this is just an example of amplification, you don't need to really understand this.</p><p><br></p><h2 id="3.4-accurate-amplification">3.4 Accurate amplification</h2><p>We of course want the amplification to be accurate, we want our output to be determined <strong>only </strong>by the input and gain, <strong>irrespective of source and load.</strong></p><p><br></p><p>In the <strong>ideal</strong> case, we would want to have a <span class="ql-formula" data-value="0\Omega">﻿<span contenteditable="false"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">Ω</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0\Omega</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.68333em;
