Given a scheduling problem: we need to perform maintenance on power units,but we want as little outage as possible. Per unit, we know its capacity and the number of intervals required for the maintenance of this unit.


When some units are in maintenance, we still want reserve power to be available: total capacity - leftover capacity - expected load. This should be ﻿≥0\ge0≥0﻿.


Encoding

A candidate solution would be a scheduled. We want to encode that multiple units can be maintained at the same time.


As we have 7 units, we encode each unit into a 4 bit string, where ﻿111﻿ means maintenance in that quarter, and a ﻿000﻿ means no maintenance. This yields chromosomes of 28 bits:

Image taken from slides by P. Murukannaiah


The genes will be the 4 bit schedules for units, they are not divisible any further.


Crossover & Mutation

Crossover should not cut across genes, so our schedules per unit will remain valid.


Mutation selects another gene from the allowed "gene pool", there will be no individual bit flipping. This "gene pool" is determined by the amount of quarters of maintenance required per unit.


Fitness function

We can use the net reserve per quarter and take the minimum value of the net reserve. If ﻿&lt;0&lt;0<0﻿, the fitness will be ﻿000﻿.
