Introduction

In swarm intelligence, the whole > the sum of the parts. Complex tasks can be tackled only through cooperation of the multitude.


The outcome of the effort comes at a group level

  • The single parts may not be aware of the bigger goal
  • There is no leader or centralized coordination
  • Interaction/coordination happens at a microlevel
  • Direct: through senses
  • Indirect: through changes in the environment


The individual, artificial ants solve optimization problems, inspired by how real ants communicate. For example, finding the shortest way can be done by:

  • Setting the same non-zero probability to be explored to all paths
  • When exploring different paths, ants release a quanity of pheromone ﻿τ\tauτ﻿, proportional to the length of the path
  • The probability that an ant chooses a specific path depends on the amount of pheromone that was deposited on it
  • Repeat until some convergence criteria

Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO)

The idea of PSO is:

  1. Separation: steer to avoid crowding local flockmates
  2. Alignment: steer toward the average heading of local flockmates
  3. Cohesion: steer to move toward the average position of local flockmates


In PSO, each particle is a possible solution, which is a vector of numbers. They all have their own position and velocity, which drives the optimization process. Their next position can be calculated based on their velocity ﻿xi(t+1)=xi(t)+vi(t+1)x_i\left(t+1\right)=x_i\left(t\right)+v_i\left(t+1\right)xi​(t+1)=xi​(t)+vi​(t+1)﻿.


A swarm of particles "flies" in a synchronized way over a hyper-dimensional solution space, targeting the best solution. Particles change their positions according to the results they achieved and the results their companions achieved.

We use a fitness function ﻿f(xi(t))f\left(x_i\left(t\right)\right)f(xi​(t))﻿, which indicates how good the solution represented by a particle is. Termination

The algorithm stops when:

  • Max number of iteration has been reached
  • A good enough solution has been found
  • There are no further changes in positions
  • Velocities are close to 0


Interaction with neighbors

  • Star topology (global best): higher connectivity between particles
  • Converges faster


  • Ring topology (local best): lower connectivity between particles
  • Neighbors are identified by predetermined indexes, sometimes based on distance
  • Information is propagated through overlapping neighborhoods
  • More diversity and less chances to get stuck in local optima
  • Computationally more expensive
