CSE Signal processing

This course is about representation and manipulation of signals

  • Signals originate as sensory data from the real (physical) world.
  • Visual images, sound waves, electronic pulses, …
  • A sensor is needed to capture the real world
  • CCD/CMOS, microphone, speed, flow, magnetometer, voltmeter, seismometer, …
  • Real world signals are time- and amplitude-continuous (or analog). To process them we need to convert them to a digital form
  • Discrete-time and amplitude discrete so we can store signal values (samples) in a (1D, 2D) array.
  • Process is called sampling or A/D conversion
